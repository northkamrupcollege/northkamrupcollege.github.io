Courses Offered

Under the aegis of A. H. S. E. C. and Gauhati University, the college offers the priviledge of imparting studies in Arts Faculty both in H. S. and Degree levels.

Higher Secondary Courses

The following subjects are offered in Higher Secondary courses in North Kamrup College.

Subjects Offered in H. S. Courses
English
M. I. L. (Assamese, Bodo)
Economics
Political Science		Education
Logic and Philosophy
Advanced Assamese
Environmental Education

Note #1

  • English and Environmental Education is compulsory.
  • A student must select one Modern Indian Language (M. I. L.).
  • A student must select three / four elective subject from the following list.
    • Economics
    • Political Science
    • History
    • Education
    • Logic and Philosophy
    • Advanced Assamese
  • Out of the four optional subjects one automatically becomes the fourth subject in which the concerned student scores the least marks

Three Years Degree Courses

According to the approval of the meeting of the Academic Council, Gauhati University, North Kamrup College is implementing Semester System in T. D. C. from session 2011-12. So a student seeking admission into T. D. C. has to complete the course in six semester (two semester per year) as formed by Gauhati University.

Schedule for Three Year Degree Courses

First, Third and Fifth Semester1 August - 31 December (Including examinations)
Second, Fourth and Sixth Semester1 January - 30 June (Including examinations)
Semester Break1 July - 31 July

A student seeking admission into the First semester may choose any of the following subjects as Major subject.

List A
Assamese
Economics
Education
History		English
Political Science
Philosophy
Bodo

A student with Major has to choose any of the following as First subsidiary subject from the following list.

List B
Philosophy
Education
Assamese
Political Sciene		History
Elective
Economics
Bodo

In addition to it, he / she has to choose M. I. L. (Assamese, Bodo) or Alternative English as Second subsidiary and English as compulsory subject for both semester 1 and 2. In semester 3 and 4, instead of English, he / she has to take Environmental Studies as compulsory subject. In semester 5 and 6, he / she has to continue Major subject only.

A student without Major has to choose any two subjects from List A as First elective and Second elective and M. I. L. (Assamese, Bodo) as Third elective including English as compulsory subject for the semester 1 and 2. In semester 3 and 4, instead of English, he / she has to take Environmental Studies as compulsory subject. In semester 5 and 6, he / she has to continue First and Second elective subjects only.
